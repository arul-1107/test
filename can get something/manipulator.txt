Description: A multi-jointed arm that mimics the movements of a human arm.
Applications: Grasping, lifting, digging, drilling, and other manipulation tasks.
Example: The Mars rovers, such as the Mars Curiosity Rover, are equipped with robotic arms.

Components:
Base:
The base serves as the attachment point for the manipulator to the rover.
It should allow for rotation, enabling the manipulator to reach different areas without moving the entire rover.

Arm:
The arm is responsible for extending and retracting the manipulator.
It can be a telescopic arm made of lightweight materials like aluminum to keep the weight minimal.

End Effector:
The end effector is the tool or device at the end of the manipulator that performs tasks.
It could be a simple gripper for grabbing objects, a camera for inspection, or any other tool suited for the mission.

Actuators:
Use simple electric motors or servo motors for actuation.
Linear actuators for extending and retracting the arm.
Rotational actuators for the base and wrist movements.

Control System:
Implement a basic control system that allows operators to manipulate the arm remotely.
Use simple joystick controls for basic movements.
Incorporate sensors for feedback to enhance precision

Mechanism:

Base Rotation:
Employ a motorized joint at the base for rotating the entire manipulator.
This allows the rover to change the direction of the manipulator without repositioning the entire vehicle.

Telescopic Arm:
Use a linear actuator to extend and retract the arm.
The telescopic design provides flexibility in reaching different distances.

Wrist Movement:
Add a rotational joint (actuator) at the wrist to allow the end effector to be positioned correctly.

End Effector Attachment:
Design the end effector attachment mechanism to be modular, allowing easy swapping of tools based on mission requirements.
